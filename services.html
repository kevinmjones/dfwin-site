How to engage

Consultancy on the strategy. Or the operations team itself.

The split is deliberate. Some category problems are answered with a study and a recommendation. Others can only be answered by someone running the program week in, week out. DFWin does both.

Track 01 / Advisory

Supply Chain Strategy Consultancy

An advisory engagement, typically 4–12 weeks. We build the sourcing strategy, assess supplier capability, model the logistics, and deliver an industry view and benchmark analysis with the math behind it. The output is a decision your team can defend internally and act on with confidence.

What you get:

  • · Sourcing strategy & supplier shortlist with capability ratings
  • · Logistics & landed-cost model
  • · Industry insight & competitive benchmark
  • · Feasibility study for a move, a new category, or a relocation
Scope an advisory engagement
Track 02 / Operations

Supply Chain Operations

An end-to-end engagement. DFWin becomes your virtual operations team in Asia — owning sourcing, supplier audits, NPI, order fulfilment, quality assurance and logistics. One team, one number to call, one P&L on the line with yours. Most of our long-term customers buy us this way.

What you get:

  • · Qualified suppliers, audited and on contract
  • · NPI and engineering through to ramp
  • · On-site QA: pre-production, in-line, pre-shipment
  • · Order management, consolidation, freight, customs & last-mile
Discuss an operations engagement

01 / Capability

Strategic sourcing & supplier capability

We audit, qualify, and benchmark factories across China and Southeast Asia, then negotiate terms that hold up under audit and through fluctuations in raw material cost. The output is not a brochure — it's a shortlist with capability ratings, a price build-up, and a contract you can sign.

  • · On-site supplier audits against your category specification
  • · Capability assessment: tooling, capacity, financial health, references
  • · Commercial negotiation, contract drafting, dual-source planning

02 / Capability

New Product Introduction & engineering

From a sketch or a marine-grade spec to a production-ready unit. Our engineers work alongside your team and the factory to design, tool, validate and ramp — without the ten emails a day, and without the back-translation losses that kill detail in cross-border NPI projects.

  • · Design-for-manufacture review and tooling strategy
  • · Engineering samples, validation builds, golden samples
  • · Ramp plan, capacity reservation, first-article inspection

03 / Capability

Quality assurance on the factory floor

Pre-production, in-line and pre-shipment inspections by DFWin QA staff — not a third-party inspector you've never met whose loyalty isn't to your spec. Issues caught in Shenzhen never become issues in Miami, Port Everglades, or on a dry-dock pier in Singapore.

  • · AQL-based inspection plans tailored to the category
  • · In-line audits during the first production runs
  • · Pre-shipment inspection with photo/video reporting

04 / Capability

Logistics & order fulfilment

Consolidation, freight, customs and last-mile to the shipyard, the dry-dock or the hotel — including the dry-dock support windows where the cargo has to land on the day, not the week. We hold the schedule and we hold the carriers to it.

  • · Multi-supplier consolidation and container loading plans
  • · Sea, air and hybrid freight depending on the call
  • · Dry-dock window delivery, with people on the pier if needed

05 / Capability

Market intelligence & feasibility

Industry insight, benchmark analysis and feasibility studies for category teams who need to decide whether a move makes sense before they commit budget to it. Our research is grounded in the same supplier relationships we use for sourcing — not a database somebody else assembled.

  • · Category & competitive benchmarks against named suppliers
  • · Country-of-origin analysis: China + 1, near-shoring options
  • · Feasibility decks for board / steering-committee review

06 / Capability

End-to-end operations — the virtual team model

Most long-term DFWin customers don't buy capabilities one at a time. They buy us as the operations team — sourcing, NPI, QA and logistics rolled into one accountable group with a single point of contact, weekly status, and a P&L on the line with theirs. It's the closest you can get to having a Hong Kong office without opening one.

  • · Named account team — buyers, engineers, QA, logistics in one room
  • · Weekly program governance with the customer's category leads
  • · Direct escalation path: one number, one accountable owner

The process

From brief to dockside, in five steps.

Every program runs through the same five stages, whether the engagement is a four-week study or a multi-year operations contract.

Step 01

Brief

Spec, volumes, target landed cost, delivery window, risk tolerance. We don't move until we agree what "good" looks like.

Step 02

Source & engineer

Supplier audits, capability scoring, NPI build, tooling, golden sample sign-off.

Step 03

QA

Pre-production, in-line and pre-shipment inspection by our staff, with photo/video reporting on every release.

Step 04

Ship

Consolidation, freight, customs and last-mile to the shipyard, the dry-dock or the warehouse.

Step 05

Support

Reorder management, continuous improvement, dry-dock support, and on-site presence when the ship needs it.
