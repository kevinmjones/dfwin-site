Case study · Celebrity Cruises · Dry dock support

On the deck plate at the Solstice dry dock in Singapore.

When Celebrity Solstice went into dry dock, our representatives went with her — to commission the new minibar fridges, walk the cabins, and absorb the urgent demand windows that decide whether a refit lands clean or late.

Client
Celebrity Cruises
Category
In-cabin minibar fridges
Location
Singapore
Scope
Dry-dock commissioning & onsite support

The brief

A dry dock is the most expensive day on a cruise ship's calendar. Every category of vendor lands cargo, every trade is competing for the same corridors, and the schedule is held together by the engineers who can answer a question on the spot. A minibar programme of this scale isn't validated by a packing list — it's validated by walking a cabin with the operator and saying yes, this unit is ready to sail.

What we did

DFWin sent its own representatives from Hong Kong to Singapore for the duration of the support window. The team handled commissioning of the minibar fridges onboard — verifying power, fit, refrigerant performance and finish — and stayed on hand for the kind of last-minute demand that only surfaces once a ship is open and crews are working through every cabin.

That presence matters because dry-dock issues don't queue up politely. A unit that needs swapping at 11pm in cabin 8412 is a problem that has to be solved by midnight, not by a ticket filed back to head office. Being on the deck plate is the only way a supplier can close that loop in the same shift.

The cargo lands on the dock. The vendor lands with it.

The result

The minibar fridge scope of the Solstice dry dock was commissioned inside its support window, with our team available throughout. The exercise also fed directly back into the broader Solstice-class programme — observations from the deck are the same observations that will shape how we ship and support the rest of the class refurbishments starting spring 2026.

Why it matters

Being closer to the supply base in China is half of the DFWin story. Being willing to fly to the yard is the other half. The customers who buy from us tend to come back not because of the unit on the pallet, but because of the team standing next to it when the gangway opens.
