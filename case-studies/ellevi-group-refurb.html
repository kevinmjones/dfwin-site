Case study · Ellevi Group · Cruise refurbishment

Minibars for a refurbishment specialist that has been outfitting cruise ships since 1976.

DFWin supplies minibar fridges to Ellevi Group — a leader in cruise refurbishment and high-quality marine furnishings — putting our sourcing and logistics capability to work for one of the industry's most respected refurb names.

The brief

Ellevi Group has been outfitting cruise ships since 1976 — refit programmes, public-area refurbishments, cabin make-overs at the scale that only a specialist refurbishment house can deliver. Their brand has been built on furnishings that hold up to the sea and to the schedules cruise operators run on.

When Ellevi needed minibar fridges for its cruise refurbishment pipeline, the requirement was specific: a premium unit that matches the quality of the rest of the cabin, delivered into refit windows that don't have slack in them.

What we did

We brought Ellevi the same proposition we bring the operators directly — a minibar unit engineered for cruise service, sourced from a factory we know intimately, inspected by our own QA team, and delivered into Europe on a schedule that matches the refit calendar.

The difference, working with a refurbishment specialist, is that the unit has to integrate into someone else's cabin design. Finish, dimensions and installation interface all have to fit Ellevi's specification, not ours. We worked the spec until the fridge dropped into the cabin like it was always meant to be there.

Refurb work is unforgiving — the new unit lives next to the operator's existing cabin language, and the seams have to disappear.

The result

DFWin is now a supply partner inside Ellevi's cruise refurbishment programme — leveraging our sourcing and logistics expertise on behalf of a specialist whose brand is built on the quality of the finished ship. Every cabin Ellevi refurbishes with a DFWin minibar is a quiet endorsement of the unit and the supply chain behind it.

Why it matters

Refurbishment is one of the most consequential and least-discussed segments of the cruise industry. Ships are refit every few years, every cabin gets touched, and the contracts go to the specialists who can deliver. Becoming a trusted supplier inside that segment is how a category like the minibar gets onto ships we never directly negotiate with — and that's the long game we are playing.
