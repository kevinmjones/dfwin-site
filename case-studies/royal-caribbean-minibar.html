Case study · Royal Caribbean Group · Fleet refit

Re-platforming the minibar across the Solstice class.

Royal Caribbean Group has awarded DFWin the minibar fridge programme for the refurbishment of its Celebrity Solstice-class ships, beginning spring 2026. A category that touches every cabin, on every sailing, for the next decade.

Client
Royal Caribbean Group
Category
In-cabin minibar fridges
Programme start
Spring 2026
Scope
Celebrity Solstice-class refurbishment

The brief

A refurbishment programme of the Solstice class is a fleet-wide event. Five ships, thousands of cabins, sailing schedules that don't bend, and a guest expectation set by the rest of a five-star cruise experience. The minibar fridge sounds like a small thing until you remember it lives in every cabin, runs 24/7, and is one of the first things a guest opens.

Royal Caribbean Group needed a partner who could supply the unit at premium quality and consistent spec across the entire class — and back the programme through commissioning, refit windows, and the years of warranty support that follow.

What we did

DFWin already had the cruise-line minibar category in its DNA. We brought engineering, sourcing, and onboard support to the table as a single offer: a fridge specified for marine conditions, factory-validated against the operator's own QA standard, packed and routed for delivery to the yard on the dry-dock window.

The award is the result of a multi-year qualification cycle — sample evaluation, on-site factory audits, and reference checks against the units already operating in the rest of RCG's fleet. The brief was simple: don't just match the previous supplier, raise the bar.

A premium in-cabin amenity, delivered the same way on every ship in the class.

The result

Starting spring 2026, every Solstice-class refurbishment slot will be supplied with DFWin minibar fridges. The contract represents a strategic shift for the operator — moving the category onto a single, accountable supply chain that can be coordinated across yards in Singapore, the Bahamas, and Europe.

Why it matters

This is the kind of win that doesn't come from a brochure. It comes from twelve years of working the cruise-line minibar category — knowing what fails in service, what surveyors flag, and how to land a container at a yard the day before a refit slot opens. The Solstice award is a vote of confidence in that operational fluency, and a programme we expect to be talking about for the rest of the decade.
