Case study · Disney Cruise Line · New-build

Taking Disney's luggage cage business away from the incumbent.

Disney Cruise Line awarded DFWin the luggage cage contracts for two new ships, Adventure and Destiny. A category previously held by another vendor — won back on quality, durability, and the willingness to engineer to Disney's spec.

Client
Disney Cruise Line
Category
Luggage cages
Vessels
Adventure & Destiny
Scope
New-build fit-out

The brief

Luggage cages are unglamorous and load-bearing in the most literal sense. They live in the working corridors of a cruise ship, get hammered turn after turn, and have to land at a new-build yard on the day a hull is ready to take them — not the week. The category had been with a competing vendor for years.

Disney's specification is its own discipline: tolerances are tight, finishes are reviewed line by line, and a vendor is expected to come to the table already understanding how the ship moves people, not just how a cage is welded.

What we did

We approached the category the way we approach every cage programme — starting from the failure modes, not the drawing. Where does the previous unit bend in service. Which welds are getting flagged at the operator's QA station. What does ten years of saltwater corrosion look like on a baseplate. Then we engineered to outperform on each, and put the math behind the price.

Our team worked with Disney's category engineers through sample, pre-production and pilot runs, validated by on-site inspection at our manufacturing partner. Tooling was prepared with both Adventure and Destiny in mind so the second ship benefits from everything we learn delivering the first.

The contract didn't move because of price. It moved because the unit was better, and the team behind it answered the phone.

The result

Two new ships, one supply chain, a category recovered from a competitor and locked in for the new-build cycle. It is a meaningful win in a corner of the cruise industry where vendor changes are rare and reputation compounds quietly over years.

Why it matters

Disney is an exacting customer for a reason — and a vendor that earns the work earns it elsewhere too. The award reinforces DFWin's position in the cruise category we know best, and validates a track record of supplying luggage cages to cruise operators since 2014.

More work

Winning the contract is one thing. Standing it up at a dry dock 12 time zones away is another.
